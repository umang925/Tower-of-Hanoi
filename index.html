What is Towers of Hanoi?

The Towers of Hanoi is a classic mathematical puzzle that consists of three rods and a number of disks of different sizes which can slide onto any rod. The puzzle starts with the disks in a neat stack in ascending order of size on one rod, the smallest at the top, thus making a conical shape.

It is said that the world will end when the puzzle is solved. Therefore, it's a race against time to see who can solve it first!

How to Play

The objective of the puzzle is to move the entire stack to another rod, obeying the following simple rules:

  1. Only one disk can be moved at a time.
  2. Each move consists of taking the upper disk from one of the stacks and placing it on top of another stack.
  3. No disk may be placed on top of a smaller disk.

It may sound easy, but don't be fooled. Many have attempted and failed!

History

The exact origin of the Towers of Hanoi puzzle is unknown. It is believed to have been invented by the French mathematician Édouard Lucas in the 19th century. However, there are also references to similar problems dating back to ancient India and China.

Over the years, the puzzle has gained popularity as a brain teaser and has been featured in various forms of media.
